Disability Royal Commission

Supporting You Through the Royal Commission

The Royal Commission into Violence, Abuse, Neglect and Exploitation of People with Disability is examining how to better protect people with disability and prevent abuse, neglect and exploitation across all settings including the home, disability services, workplaces, schools and the justice system.

DAS is here to support people with disability to understand the Royal Commission process and engage with this important national conversation.

What is the Royal Commission?

The Royal Commission investigates systemic failures in preventing and responding to violence, abuse, neglect and exploitation of people with disability. Key focus areas include:

  • How abuse happens and why systems fail to prevent it
  • Barriers preventing people with disability from reporting abuse
  • Gaps in laws and policies that should protect people
  • How organizations and institutions respond to complaints
  • What systemic changes are needed to better protect people with disability

How DAS Can Help

DAS provides support including:

  • Information: Clear, accessible information about the Royal Commission and its recommendations
  • Advocacy: Support in understanding your rights and how the recommendations affect you
  • Awareness: Community workshops and information sessions
  • Navigation: Help engaging with government consultations and implementation processes
  • Voice: Supporting you to share your experiences and advocate for change

Key Resources

For more information about the Royal Commission, visit the official website at disability.royalcommission.gov.au

The final report includes 222 recommendations covering:

  • Prevention of abuse, neglect and exploitation
  • Recognition and reporting of incidents
  • Response and support for victims
  • Accountability and justice
  • Systemic change across government and organizations
