Privacy Statement

Our Commitment to Privacy

Disability Advocacy Service Inc. (DAS) is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information responsibly. This Privacy Statement explains how we collect, use, and protect your information.

What Information We Collect

We may collect personal information including:

  • Name and contact details
  • Information about your disability or support needs
  • Details about advocacy matters you need support with
  • Feedback and communication with us
  • Website usage information

How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Provide advocacy services and support
  • Communicate with you about services and events
  • Respond to inquiries and feedback
  • Improve our services
  • Meet legal and regulatory obligations

Confidentiality

DAS operates under strict confidentiality principles:

  • All client information is kept confidential
  • Information is only shared with your consent
  • Staff are bound by confidentiality requirements
  • We comply with privacy legislation

Data Security

We take appropriate security measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse. This includes:

  • Secure storage of client files and records
  • Limited access to personal information
  • Regular security reviews
  • Secure disposal of records

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal information
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information (where appropriate)
  • Lodge a complaint if you believe your privacy has been breached

Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Statement or concerns about how we handle your information, please contact us:

Phone: (08) 8953-1422
Email: admin@das.org.au

Updates to This Statement

We may update this Privacy Statement from time to time. We will notify you of any significant changes by updating the date at the bottom of this statement.
